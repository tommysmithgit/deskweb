Water Apple

Syzygium samarangense is a plant species in the family Myrtaceae, native to an area that includes the Greater Sunda Islands, Malay Peninsula and the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, but introduced in prehistoric times to a wider area[2] and now widely cultivated in the tropics. Common names in English include wax apple,[2] Java apple, Semarang rose-apple and wax jambu.[3]
